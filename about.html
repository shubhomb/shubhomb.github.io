  Personal

Hello and welcome to Shubhom.com, my personal site! I'm a freshman at Cornell University in Ithaca, New York. I grew up in metro-Atlanta and went to Alpharetta High School, located in suburban Atlanta. My dad is from Kolkata, India, a beautiful city that I visited almost every summer growing up, while my mom is from Durgapur, a pleasantly less-populated city about 3 hours away by car. As a result of my Bengali heritage, I'm fluent in the Bengali language, though I'm borderline illiterate when it comes to reading and writing. I also know French (bienvenue à ma site personnel!). This site was a personal project that began the summer after my senior year in high school, as a way to explore web design in a functional and creative way. Take a look around and contact me if you have questions, concerns, or just want to chat!

  Work

My research interests include organic and flexible electronics. One of the reasons I am pursuing an electrical engineering degree is the range of potential applications that modern engineering encompasses, including medicine, transportation, and communication. The first area that attracted me to engineering was renewable energy. Renewable energy has been perhaps the most dangerous threat to humanity for my generation, as a result of excessive consumption of fossil fuels. To address this, my first research project as a high schooler working at Georgia Tech’s Kippelen Lab attempted to make a transparent organic solar cell . The objective in creating organic photovoltaic devices is to make mass-producible, environmentally-friendly, and efficient solar cells. This research introduced me to another fascinating aspect of research today: its interdisciplinary nature. Teams of chemists, electrical engineers, and doctors (among a plethora of other disciplines) collaborate to develop new technologies, which allows for great versatility. Now that I’m in college, I look forward to continuing my work with renewables and expanding my focus to other applications. Outside research, I like discussing just about anything. I like reading about history and geopolitics because I believe understanding these gives a fresh perspective that’s not only essential to connecting with other cultures but also pivotal to understanding ourselves. Computer science also fascinates me in its ability to harness creativity and problem-solving skills to produce cool new things, like this website.
